Topology Metrics.

A topological analysis is performed that determines if the model is manifold, composed of a single complete solid or shell, or non-manifold. Non-manifold objects may include a mixture of shell and solid elements, or they may be objects that are not correctly closed. Non-manifold edges may indicate the presence of hinges in the model.

The command window prints a guess of the topology of the model. For example, the topology information for figure * is calculated as: 

----------------------------
Mesh Topology Information
----------------------------
   Probable Topology:  Open Surface with 11 hole(s)
   Possible Topologies: N Open surfaces with N+10 hole(s)

This calculation is based on the Euler-Descartes formula E= v - e + f, where v is the number of vertices, e is the number of edges, and f is the number of faces[3].

Verde 2.5 can display non-manifold edges as shown in figure *. A non-manifold edge occurs when more than two entities form part of a boundary edge. The figure shows two blocks that are only connected along one edge. This is not a valid solid object. If the model is not properly constrained, it could result in a hinge that may cause some analysis codes to fail.
